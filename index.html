Intraday Stock Price Explorer

Finding Daily Correlations

Greg Graham

Introduction

If a stock price opens down, does it come back up at a regular time during the day? Does it continue down? Are there subsequent price movements that are correlated with an opening price movement? Or are subsequent movements uncorrelated?

Using the Intraday Stock Price Explorer you can:

  • Create an ensemble of daily price movements for up to 28 days sampled at 10, 15, or 30 minute intervals.
  • Display these price movements overlaid on each other with stacked volume charts
  • Look for correlations of later price movements relative to the open over the sample of days collected.
  • Explore daily price movements by Varying the correlation reference period away from the open, and get a feel for trading activity.

The application uses an undocumented Google Finance API for intraday prices called getprices. It also uses rCharts and Shiny.

Stock Symbol: XOM, 6 days Prices at 30 minute intervals

  • Click on individual days on the legend to remove that day from the plot.
  • Double-click on individual days on the legend to remove all days but the selected days from the plot.

  • Identify time periods with the most trading activity, significant because a strategy is more likely to be well-executed during those times.

Stock Symbol: XOM, correlations to the open at 15 minute intervals

  • The plot on the left ("Rolling") shows the correlation of the price movement at the open with subsequent price movements
  • The plot on the right ("Fixed") shows the correlation of the price movement at the open with cumulative price movement at each point during the day.
  • In the full application, the reference period can be varied away from the open.
